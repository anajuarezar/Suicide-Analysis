Understanding World Suicide Patterns


Suicide is a serious global public health issue. Globally, around 703 000 people die by suicide every year and it is among the leading causes of death worldwide, having a higher death count than malaria, HIV/AIDS, breast cancer, or war and homicide.
More than in every 100 deaths (1.3%) in 2019 were the result of suicide according to the World Health Organization.

Social, psychological, cultural and other factors can interact leading a person to present suicidal behaviour. But one of the main problems is the stigma that is connected to mental disorders and suicide, meaning that many people don't look for help. With timely interventions, suicide is preventable, but usually mental health and suicide are on the bottom of the list as a priority for governments and policy-makers.
